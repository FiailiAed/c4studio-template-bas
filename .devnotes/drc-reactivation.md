# Direct-Response Copywriter - Reactivation Campaign

Here's the full campaign for Athletes United, written in Coach Colin's voice — no agency polish, no name-merge openers. Two placeholders run through it: `[RAFFLE_LINK]` (where email readers enter) and `[BOOKING_LINK]` (where a YES reply routes them to schedule).

---

## 1. THE REACTIVATION OFFER

Win a brand-new men's lacrosse stick plus a free 1-on-1 session with Coach Colin — a $200 prize — just for getting your athlete's name back in the mix this month. One entry, no cost, one winner.

---

## 2. DAY 1 — Initial Message

**SMS** *(137 characters)*
> Coach Colin here from Athletes United. It's been a while! Giving away a men's stick + free 1-on-1 ($200). Want your athlete in? Reply YES

**EMAIL**
**Subject:** There's a new stick with your athlete's name on it

> Coach Colin here from Athletes United.
>
> It's been about six months since we had your player out for a session, and I've been meaning to reach out. We're running something this month: one player walks away with a brand-new men's lacrosse stick and a free 1-on-1 with me — $200 total.
>
> Getting an entry takes about ten seconds. No catch, no cost.
>
> **→ [Enter the raffle]([RAFFLE_LINK])**
>
> (Or just reply YES and I'll get you in.)
>
> — Coach Colin
> Athletes United Lacrosse

---

> **→ [Enter the raffle]([RAFFLE_LINK])** — or reply YES.

## 5. "YES" RESPONSE HANDLER

> Boom — you're entered for the men's stick + free 1-on-1 ($200). Last step: lock in a time for your athlete's session here so you're ready to roll either way → [BOOKING_LINK]. See you on the field. — Coach Colin

---

## 6. "NO / STOP" RESPONSE HANDLER

> No problem at all — you're off this list and won't hear from me about the raffle again. If your athlete ever wants to get back out with us, you know where to find me. Take care. — Coach Colin

*(A hard "STOP" should also trigger your platform's full unsubscribe to stay TCPA-compliant; the message above is the courtesy confirmation either way.)*

---

**Two quick implementation notes:**

Append `Txt STOP to opt out` to the **first** SMS only — it keeps you compliant without cluttering every message. And if your CRM has it, swapping "your athlete" / "your player" for an `[ATHLETE_NAME]` field makes these land even harder — just keep it mid-sentence, never as a "Hi {name}!" opener. For the roughly one-in-five leads who *never* booked, the copy still works as written; if you want, I can spin a lightly different Day 1 for that segment that drops the "six months since we had your player" line, since it doesn't apply to them.
